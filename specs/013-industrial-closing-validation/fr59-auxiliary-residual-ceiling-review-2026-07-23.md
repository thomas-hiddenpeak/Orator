1. `ref-0063`, `ref-0135`, `ref-0171`, `ref-0298`, and `ref-0409` expose
   evidence already present in the accepted producer or a missing text unit;
   they are not new auxiliary producer topologies.
2. `ref-0221`, `ref-0241`, and `ref-0457` demonstrate that an auxiliary
   preference can add conflict, merge a valid handoff, or remove a useful
   primary island. They require abstention.
3. `ref-0341`, `ref-0506`, and `ref-0537` have no auxiliary activity from the
   expected speaker at the focus and cannot be repaired by this profile.
4. `ref-0239` is the only new noncritical activity complement. It has a
   writable aligned response, but its short direct query is not a causal Xu
   identity. The neighboring `ref-0241` conversation also shows that auxiliary
   local slots cannot be mapped to names by slot number.
5. `ref-0499` remains the one critical activity complement. Its new activity
   overlaps a conflicting slot, spans only part of the retained business
   source, and has no causal Shi identity. The frozen final FR50 registry is
   future state for empty-registry Run A and cannot repair that contract.

Consequently, there is no second independent residual that joins `ref-0499`
with all four required properties: genuinely new activity, a writable business
boundary, causal global identity, and accepted-control abstention. This is a
human contextual-semantic decision derived from the complete conversations,
not a profile score or an automated comparison.
